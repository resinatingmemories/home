The casting of your floral arrangement in resin can take up to 3–4 weeks. After that, I will demold, sand, and buff the piece, which is the final stage of the preservation process. The total process takes approximately 4–6 weeks.

Since each piece is hand-cast, there might be small imperfections, such as particles from flower shedding. These unique characteristics add to the charm of your custom piece.

Yes, your flowers will go through an extensive drying process that may cause them to change in quality and texture. It's normal for flowers to become more brittle and for their hues to fade. Certain flowers may show translucent spots once placed in resin.

Scratches are a natural part of working with resin, as it is a hard plastic. To clean your piece and minimize scratches, we recommend using a microfiber cloth along with soap and water or isopropyl alcohol. Avoid abrasive materials that could cause further damage.

The cloudy lines you see are typically the result of micro bubbles forming during the curing process. While we take measures to minimize bubble formation by controlling the temperature, some micro bubbles may still occur due to the natural properties of resin. These are a common characteristic and do not affect the integrity of your piece.

While many resin pieces are UV-resistant, prolonged exposure to direct sunlight can lead to fading or yellowing. For outdoor use, we recommend checking the specific properties of your item and taking care to bring it indoors when not in use. Exposing your pieces to extreme heat or direct sunlight for prolonged periods can cause fading or warping.
